IoT Enabled Fish Feeding and Water Quality Monitoring System


The IoT Enabled Fish Feeding and Water Quality Monitoring System is an innovative solution designed to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of aquaculture practices. With the rapid advancement of Internet of Things (IoT) technology, our project aims to address the critical challenges faced by fish farmers, including feeding accuracy, resource management, and environmental monitoring. This system not only automates fish feeding but also continuously monitors water quality parameters, ensuring a healthy environment for fish growth and productivity. By integrating IoT technologies, we can achieve smarter aquaculture practices, leading to sustainable fish farming and improved yields.

Project Explanation


for Choosing This Project- We chose to develop the IoT Enabled Fish Feeding and Water Quality Monitoring System due to the increasing global demand for fish and sustainable aquaculture practices. Traditional methods of fish feeding are often inefficient, leading to overfeeding or underfeeding, which can adversely affect fish health and water quality. Additionally, monitoring water parameters manually is labor-intensive and prone to errors. Our project aims to leverage IoT technology to provide real-time data and automated solutions, addressing these issues effectively while promoting responsible fish farming.

Project Objectives- This project will implement an automated fish feeding system that operates based on real-time data from water quality sensors. The system will monitor critical parameters such as pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, and turbidity, sending alerts and notifications to farmers via a mobile application. By ensuring optimal feeding schedules and maintaining ideal water conditions, we aim to enhance fish growth rates, reduce feed wastage, and ultimately improve overall farm productivity.
